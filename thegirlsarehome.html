The Girls Are Home, 2016

(A collaboration with Char Stiles) For me, this started with a keen interest in the relationship between my fading, fallible memories and a medium that boasts an ability to capture "reality". I modeled the house from memory of my childhood home and we inserted steroscopic footage behind the windows so that a viewer could peek into our daily lives. The footage is a mundane represenation of domestic life shot in our own home. As a form of non-linear, immersive storytelling I wanted to place the viewer into the story and give them a role (as a giant, voyeuristic intruder).

This piece was exhibited at WEIRD REALITY (an Art and Code symposium) at Carnegie Mellon University.
