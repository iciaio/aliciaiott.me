Short Games (2015)

These were games created in a game design course taught by Tom Corbett at CMU. I collaborated with Mackenzie Bates, Anton Pleshakov, Greg Rose, Sarah Chen, Daniel Kim to create these games. My roles included asset creation, interaction planning, storyboarding, and experience design.

Little Red in the Hood

This was a sin city inspired 2.5D stealth game based loosely off of the little red riding hood fairy tale. The player controls Little Red, a drug dealer for her trap queen grandmother. The goal is to avoid being seen by the police while completing drop-offs. My main role in this project was planning the virtual city and creating assets (buildings/cars/lights/traffic cones/etc.) for it.

Rightfully Accused (Cluedoku)

A loose rebranding of sudoku such that each row and column need to contain a villian, and location, a weapon, and a victim which tell the story of a crime commited. The layout is inspired by a film noir detective's desk. The player solves crimes by completing the puzzle. I took the artistic lead on this project and made most of the assets.
